## 4. Patterns of the Top 10%

The best sites consistently do these 5 things:

### 1. **Name the outcome, not the service**
"Start your business with confidence" beats "LLC filing services." Every winner leads with transformation, not transaction.

### 2. **Pricing visible, not hidden behind a form**
Every top-5 site shows tier pricing on the homepage or one click deep. BST currently buries the $558/$750 in a mid-page checkout button — a conversion killer.

### 3. **Show social proof where friction spikes**
Review counts and star ratings appear at the top of the nav, near CTAs, and inside pricing cards — not just in a testimonials section at the bottom.

### 4. **Three pillars, never more**
Start, Protect, Grow. Form, File, Forward. The top sites reduce complexity to exactly three ideas. BST currently lists 10+ services flat — needs hierarchy.

### 5. **Prove humanity with faces and names**
Northwest names its "200 Corporate Guides." LegalZoom shows lifestyle photos of real entrepreneurs. BST's biggest untapped asset is **local Texas, family-run, physical-binder delivery** — which lives or dies on showing actual humans and actual binders.

## 7. Design Direction Recommendation

Avoid the generic teal/blue SaaS look. BST's niche advantage is editorial, authoritative, and Texas-grounded — which calls for:

- **Warm, paper-toned background** (editorial, not clinical)
- **Deep navy + warm gold** (trust + premium physical product)
- **Burnt orange accent** (Texas heritage + CTA pattern-break)
- **Serif heading + sans body** (established firm, not startup)
- **Real photography of the binder and the team** (humanity the SaaS competitors fake)
- **Subtle paper grain texture** (pairs with the physical-binder narrative)
